GODIVA Sphere Neutronics Simulation

Validation of a Monte Carlo Model for Fast Spectrum Criticality

Introduction

This project models and analyzes the GODIVA critical assembly – a bare sphere of highly enriched uranium (HEU) – using OpenMC with the ENDF/B-VIII.0 nuclear data library. GODIVA is a well-documented benchmark used to validate neutronics codes and nuclear data in fast spectrum criticality scenarios.

The objective is to reproduce the benchmark effective multiplication factor (keff), compare results against trusted MCNP6.2 simulations, and analyze neutron flux distributions.

Methodology

Geometry

The GODIVA assembly is modeled as a bare sphere of HEU, with radius 8.741 cm.

Material Composition

  • 94.73 at% U-235
  • 5.27 at% U-238
  • Density: 18.74 g/cm3

Simulation Parameters

OpenMC k-eigenvalue setup with 10k particles, 10 inactive and 110 active cycles. A monoenergetic and then Watt-distributed neutron source were used. Shannon entropy was used for convergence verification.

Improvements

Increasing particles per cycle to 100k improved precision, with final keff = 1.00096 ± 0.00023. A gradient source model was explored, though not necessary given convergence results.
